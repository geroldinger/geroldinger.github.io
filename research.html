RESEARCH AREAS:

Number theory and commutative algebra. My current research interests focus on


Non-unique factorizations in monoids and integral domains:
The main objective of factorization theory is a systematic treatment of phenomena related to the non-uniqueness of factorizations in structures of arithmetical interest. The main focus areas are principal and non-principal orders in algebraic number and function fields, Mori domains, Krull monoids and congruence monoids. The methods are algebraic (key word: multiplicative ideal theory), combinatorial (key word: zero-sum problems) and analytic (key word: abstract analytic number theory). For more information see a recent survey paper (jointly with F. Halter-Koch) or the monograph

Non-Unique Factorizations: Algebraic, Combinatorial and Analytic Theory,
by A. Geroldinger and F. Halter-Koch, Chapman & Hall/CRC, 2006

Additive group theory:
Starting from the classical addition theorems in additive number theory additive group theory saw a rapid development during the last decades initiated by the work of P. Erdös, G.A. Freiman, M. Kneser and H.B. Mann. At present we concentrate on direct and inverse zero-sum problems over abelian groups. For more information see a survey paper (jointly with W. Gao), a lecture (held at CANT2005) or an article written for the Advanced Course in Combinatorics and Geometry: Additive Combinatorics, Barcelona 2008.

Multiplicative ideal theory:
The main objective of multiplicative ideal theory is the description of the multiplicative structure of commutative monoids and domains by means of ideals or certain systems of ideals. A modern treatment of multiplicative ideal theory, valid for both commutative rings and monoids, in the language of ideal systems on commutative monoids is given by

Ideal Systems: An Introduction to Multiplicative Ideal Theory,
by F. Halter-Koch

For a recent conferrence proceedings see

Multiplicative Ideal Theory in Commutative Algebra, Springer 2006


At present we investigate the (arithmetic and ideal theoretic) structure of congruence monoids and C-monoids (which are generalizations of Krull monoids). For recent papers see Congruence monoids (jointly with F. Halter-Koch),  Monotone Chains of Factorizations in C-monoids (jointly with A. Foroutan) and Arithmetic of Mori domains and monoids (jointly with W. Hassler).
